DELISH DISH FROSTINGS

Whipped Cream Frosting
(Makes about 2 Cups)
  • 1 Cup Cold Heavy Whipping Cream
  • ½ tsp Vanilla
  • 1-2 tsp Sugar
  1. Place your mixing bowl and wire whisk in the freezer for about 15 - 30 minutes.
  2. Place all the ingredients in the cold bowl and beat just until stiff peaks form.
  3. If not using immediately, cover and refrigerate.

Raspberry Whipped Cream Frosting
(Makes about 2 Cups)
  • Make Whipped Cream Frosting
  • Add ⅓ Cup Chilled Raspberry Puree or Seedless Raspberry Jam
  1. Make the whipped cream frosting but beat only until very soft peaks form.
  2. Add the raspberry puree or jam and beat just until stiff peaks form.
  3. Taste and fold in more sugar or puree, as needed.
  4. If not using immediately, cover and refrigerate.

Chocolate Whipped Cream Frosting
(Makes about 2 Cups)
  • ½ tsp Vanilla
  • 3-4 tsp Sugar
  • 2 tbsp Unsweetened Cocoa Powder
  • 1 Cup Cold Heavy Whipping Cream
  1. Place the vanilla extract, 3 tablespoons of sugar, and cocoa powder in the cold bowl.
  2. Stir in about 2 tablespoons of the cream to form a thick paste (this will dissolve the cocoa powder).
  3. Add the rest of the cream and beat just until stiff peaks form.
  4. If not using immediately, cover and refrigerate.

Mocha Whipped Cream Frosting
(Makes about 2 Cups)
  • ½ tsp Vanilla            
  • 3-4 tsp Sugar
  • 2 tbsp Unsweetened Cocoa Powder
  • 1 tsp Instant Coffee Powder or Espresso Powder
  • 1 Cup Cold Heavy Whipping Cream
  1. Place your mixing bowl and wire whisk in the freezer for about 15 - 30 minutes.
  2. Place the vanilla extract, 3 tablespoons of sugar, cocoa powder, and coffee powder in the cold bowl.
  3. Stir in about 2 tablespoons of the cream to form a thick paste (this will dissolve the cocoa powder and coffee powder).
  4. Add the rest of the cream and beat just until stiff peaks form.
  5. If not using immediately, cover and refrigerate.

Mango Frosting
(For Pineapple Cupcakes)
  • 1-2 Mangoes
  • 1 Cup Butter, Room Temp
  • 1 lb Box of Powdered Sugar
  • 1 tsp Vanilla
  1. Cut Mangoes. Click Here For Example.
  2. Place mango pieces in food processor and puree until you have about ⅓ cup.
  3. Beat butter and vanilla until creamy.
  4. Add powdered sugar in several additions, beating until well combined with each addition.
  5. Add ½ cup of mango puree and beat until completely combined.
  6. Spread on cooled cupcakes.
  7. (Optional Step)When the cupcakes are cool and frosted, dip them in a bowl of finely ground/crushed graham cracker crumbs.

Peanut Butter Buttercream Frosting
  • 1 Cup Unsalted Butter, Room Temp
  • 1 Heaping Cup Creamy Peanut Butter
  • 3 Cups Powdered Sugar
  • ½ Cup Heavy Cream
  • Pinch of Salt
  1. Cream together the peanut butter and butter for 2 - 3 minutes. You want the color to lighten by a few shades.
  2. Add the powdered sugar, scrape the sides of the bowl and mix until combined.
  3. Pour in the heavy cream and beat until smooth. Scrape the bowl again.
  4. Mix in the salt.
  5. Beat on high for 3 - 4 minutes or until the buttercream has become light and fluffy.
  6. Use immediately.

Classic Vanilla Buttercream Frosting
(Makes about 2½ Cups)
  • 1 Cup Unsalted Butter (2 Sticks or ½ lb.), Room Temp
  • 3-4 Cups Powdered Sugar, Sifted
  • ¼ tsp Table Salt
  • 1 tsp Vanilla Extract
  • Up to 4 Tbsp Milk or Heavy Cream.
  1. Beat butter for a few minutes with a mixer on medium speed.
  2. Add 3 Cups of powdered sugar and turn your mixer on the lowest speed (so the sugar doesn't go everywhere) until the sugar has been incorporated with the butter.
  3. Increase mixer speed to medium and add vanilla, salt, and 2 tbsp of milk/cream and beat for 3 minutes.
  • ⚠ If the frosting needs to have a stiffer consistency, add remaining sugar ⚠
  • ⚠ If the frosting needs to be thinned out, add remaining milk 1 tbsp at a time ⚠

Chocolate Brownie Frosting
(Makes about 1⅔ Cups For Chocolate Brownies)
  • 6 tbsp Butter, Room Temp
  • 6 tbsp Cocoa
  • 1 tbsp Light Corn Syrup or Honey
  • 1 tsp Vanilla Extract
  • 2 Cups Powdered Sugar
  • 2-4 tbsp milk
  1. Beat butter, cocoa, corn syrup (or honey) in small bowl until blended.
  2. Add powdered sugar and milk; beat to spreading consistancy
