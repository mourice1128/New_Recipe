Pineapple Cupcakes
  • 2½ Cups Flour
  • 2 tsp Baking Powder
  • ½ tsp Baking Soda
  • ¼ tsp Salt
  • ½ Cup Unsalted Butter, Room Temp
  • 1½ Cups Sugar
  • 3 Eggs, Room Temp
  • 1 tsp Vanilla
  • ½ Cup Milk, Room Temp
  • 20 Oz. Can of Crushed Pineapple, Drained & Pureed
  1. Preheat oven to 350° and line cupcake tray with 24 paper liners.
  2. Whisk together the flour, baking powder, baking soda and salt in a medium bowl and set aside.
  3. Cream the butter and sugar together in a mixture for several minutes.
  4. Add the eggs one at a time, scrapping down the sides of the bowl with each addition.
  5. Add the vanilla and mix until combined.
  6. Add the flour mixture to the sugar mixture in three additions with the milk in two additions (flour, milk, flour, milk, flour).
  7. Mix until combined with each addition.
  8. Add the pineappleand mix until combined.
  9. Fill cups about ⅔ full and bake for about 15 minutes.

Yellow Cake
  • 2⅓ Cups Flour
  • 1 tbsp Baking Powder
  • ¾ tsp Salt
  • 1½ Cups Sugar
  • ½ Cup Shortening
  • 2 Eggs
  • 1 Cup Milk
  • 1 tsp Vanilla
  1. Pre-heat oven to 350°.
  2. Sift together flour, baking powder, salt, and sugar.
  3. Cut in shortening until fine crumbs are formed.
  4. Add eggs, milk, and vanilla. Beat at low speed for 1 minute, then high for 2 minutes, scraping the bowl frequently.
  5. Pour into 9x13" pan and bake for 25 - 30 minutes.

Pineapple Upside-Down Cake
  • 2⅓ Cups Flour
  • 1 tbsp Baking Powder
  • ¾ tsp Salt
  • 1½ Cups Sugar
  • ½ Cup Butter
  • ½ Cup Shortening
  • 2 Eggs
  • 1 Cup Milk
  • 1 tsp Vanilla
  • ⅔ Cup Brown Sugar
  • 1 Can Pineapple Rings
  1. Pre-heat oven to 350°.
  2. Sift together flour, baking powder, salt, and sugar.
  3. Cut in shortening until fine crumbs are formed.
  4. Add eggs, milk, and vanilla. Beat at low speed for 1 minute, then high for 2 minutes, scraping the bowl frequently.
  5. Melt butter in the bottom of 9x13" pan, add brown sugar and stir into butter.
  6. Arrange pinapple rings in the bottom of pan
  7. Pour into 9x13" pan and bake for 30 - 35 minutes.
  8. Cool for 5 minutes, then invert and serve.

Whipped Cream Frosting
(Makes about 2 Cups)
  • 1 Cup Cold Heavy Whipping Cream
  • ½ tsp Vanilla
  • 1-2 tsp Sugar
  1. Place your mixing bowl and wire whisk in the freezer for about 15 - 30 minutes.
  2. Place all the ingredients in the cold bowl and beat just until stiff peaks form.
  3. If not using immediately, cover and refrigerate.

Chocolate Whipped Cream Frosting
(Makes about 2 Cups)
  • ½ tsp Vanilla
  • 3-4 tsp Sugar
  • 2 tbsp Unsweetened Cocoa Powder
  • 1 Cup Cold Heavy Whipping Cream
  1. Place your mixing bowl and wire whisk in the freezer for about 15 - 30 minutes.
  2. Place the vanilla extract, 3 tablespoons of sugar, and cocoa powder in the cold bowl.
  3. Stir in about 2 tablespoons of the cream to form a thick paste (this will dissolve the cocoa powder).
  4. Add the rest of the cream and beat just until stiff peaks form.
  5. If not using immediately, cover and refrigerate.

Peanut Butter Buttercream Frosting
  • 1 Cup Unsalted Butter, Room Temp
  • 1 Heaping Cup Creamy Peanut Butter
  • 3 Cups Powdered Sugar
  • ½ Cup Heavy Cream
  • Pinch of Salt
  1. Cream together the peanut butter and butter for 2 - 3 minutes. You want the color to lighten by a few shades.
  2. Add the powdered sugar, scrape the sides of the bowl and mix until combined.
  3. Pour in the heavy cream and beat until smooth. Scrape the bowl again.
  4. Mix in the salt.
  5. Beat on high for 3 - 4 minutes or until the buttercream has become light and fluffy.
  6. Use immediately.

Classic Vanilla Buttercream Frosting
(Makes about 2½ Cups)
  • 1 Cup Unsalted Butter (2 Sticks or ½ lb.), Room Temp
  • 3-4 Cups Powdered Sugar, Sifted
  • ¼ tsp Table Salt
  • 1 tsp Vanilla Extract
  • Up to 4 Tbsp Milk or Heavy Cream.
  1. Beat butter for a few minutes with a mixer on medium speed.
  2. Add 3 Cups of powdered sugar and turn your mixer on the lowest speed (so the sugar doesn't go everywhere) until the sugar has been incorporated with the butter.
  3. Increase mixer speed to medium and add vanilla, salt, and 2 tbsp of milk/cream and beat for 3 minutes.
  • ⚠ If the frosting needs to have a stiffer consistency, add remaining sugar ⚠
  • ⚠ If the frosting needs to be thinned out, add remaining milk 1 tbsp at a time ⚠

Chocolate Brownies
  • 1 Cup Butter (2 Sticks or ½ lb.)
  • 2 Cups Sugar
  • 2 tsp Vanilla Extract
  • 4 Eggs
  • ¾ Cup Cocoa
  • 1 Cup Flour
  • ½ tsp Baking Powder
  • ¼ tsp Salt
  • 1 Cup Chopped Nuts (Optional)
  • Creamy Brownie Frosting (Optional)
  1. Heat oven to 350°.
  2. Grease 13x9x2" baking pan.
  3. Place butter in a lg. microwave-safe bowl. Microwave on MEDIUM (50%) for 2 - 2½ minutes or until melted.
  4. Stir in sugar and vanilla.
  5. Add eggs, one at a time, beating well with spoon after each addition.
  6. Add cocoa; beat until well blended.
  7. Add flour, baking powder, and salt; beat well.
  8. Stir in nuts (Optional)
  9. Pour batter into prepared pan.
  10. Bake 30 - 35 minutes or until brownies begin to pull away from the sides.
  11. Cool completely in pan on wire rack.
  12. Prepare frosting and frost (Optional)
  • ⚠ You can find the brownie frosting in the Frostings section. ⚠

America's Classic No-Fail Chocolate Chip Cookies
2¼ Cups Flour
1 tsp Baking Soda
1 tsp Salt
1 Cup Butter, Melted Then Cooled
 1 Cup Sugar
½ Cup Brown Sugar
2 Eggs, Slightly Beaten
2 tsp Vanilla
2 Cups Semisweet Chocolate Chips
  1. Preheat oven to 375°
  2. Combine flour, baking soda, and salt in a medium bowl; set aside.
  3. Beat butter, sugar, brown sugar by hand in large bowl, until combined.
  4. Add eggs and vanilla; beat until well blended.
  5. Add flour mixture, mix until combined.
  6. Stir in chocolate chips.
  7. Drop cookie dough by rounded tbsp onto ungreased cookie sheet about 1 - 1½" apart.
  8. Bake 12 - 14 minutes or until edges are crisp, but centers are still soft.
  9. Let stand on cookie sheet about 2 minutes on cookie sheet prior to moving to wire cooling rack.

Soft Cut-Out Sugar Cookies
¾ Unsalted Butter, Sofened to Room Temp
¾ Cup Sugar
1 Lg. Egg, Room Temp
2 tsp Vanilla Extract
 ¼ tsp Almond Extract
2¼ Flour
½ tsp Baking Powder
¼ tsp Salt                                        
  1. In large bowl, using the paddle mixer attachment, beat butter until smooth; about 1 minute.
  2. Add sugar and beat on high speed until light and fluffy; about 3 - 4 minutes.
  3. Add the egg, vanilla extract, and almond extract and beat on high until fully combined; about 2 minutes.
    ★Scrape down the sides and bottom of the bowl as needed.★
  4. Whisk the flour, baking powder, and salt together in a medium bowl.
  5. Turn mixer to low speed and add about ½ of the flour mixture, beating until just barely combined.
    ★If the dough seems too soft, add 1 Tbsp more of flour until better consistency.★
  6. Divide dough into 2 equal parts.
  7. Roll each portion out onto a piece of parchment paper about ¼" thick.
  8. Stack the pieces (with paper) onto a baking sheet; refridgerate at least 1 hr, but no more than 1 day.
    ★Chilling is mandatory.★
    ★If chilling for more than a couple hrs, top with a single piece of parchment paper.★
  9. Once chilled, preheat oven to 375°
  10. Line 2 - 3 large baking sheets with parchment paper.
    ★The amount of batches will depend on how big/small you cut the cookies.★
  11. Remove one of the dough pieces from the refridgerator; using a cookie cutter cut into shapes.
  12. Transfer the cut cookie dough to the prepared baking sheet; re-roll the dough and repeat until dough is gone.
  13. (Optional Step) Add sprinkles to dough prior to baking.
  14. Bake 8 - 11 minutes until very lightly colored on top and around edges.
    ★Rotate the baking sheet halfway through bake time.★
  15. Allow to cool on baking sheet for 5 minutes; transfer to wire rack to cool completely prior to icing.
★For not so sweet cookies, you can add a little salt to the mix.★

Pepperoni Spaghetti Bake
No-Stick Cooking Spray

 2 Cans (14.5 oz. each) Chef Boyardee® Spaghetti & Meatballs
¾ Cup Shredded Mozzarella Cheese, Divided

Ravioli Lasagna
No-Stick Cooking Spray
2 Cans (15 oz. each)Chef Boyardee® Beef Ravioli®
1 Cup Frozen Loose Cut Leaf Spinach
1 Can (14.5 oz.) Diced Tomatoes, Drained
 1½ tsp Dried Italian Seasoning
1 Cup Cottage Cheese
½ tsp Ground Black Pepper
1 Cup Shredded Italian Blend Cheese
  1. Preheat oven to 375°.
  2. Spray 8x8" glass baking dish with cooking spray.
  3. Place spinach in microwave-safe bowl; cover with plastic wrap and microwave on HIGH 2 minutes; squeeze spinach dry.
  4. Combine ravioli, drained tomatoes and Italian seasoning in medium microwave-safe bowl; cover with plastic wrap and microwave on HIGH 3 - 4 minutes or until mixture is hot.
  5. Combine cottage cheese, spinach, and black pepper in small bowl.
  6. Place ½ of ravioli mixture in bottom of baking dish. Top with ½ of cheese mixture and ½ cup shredded cheese; repeat layers.
  7. Bake 15 minutes or until cheese is hot and bubbly.

Lemonade
(Makes 6 - 8 Servings)
1 Cup Sugar
1 Cup Water
Rind of 2 Lemons, Cut Into Pieces
 1 Cup Lemon Juice (5 or 6 Lemons)
4 Cups Ice Water
  1. Combine sugar, 1 Cup of non-iced water, and rinds in saucepan.
  2. Stir over low heat until sugar is dissolved
  3. Boil about 7 minutes.
  4. Let cool completely.
  5. Add lemon juice and iced water.
  6. Remove rinds, stir lemonade.
  7. Pour over ice in pither or tall glasses.
Variations:
  • Quick Lemonade:
    Squeeze lemons, add sugar, water, and ice to taste

  • Minted Lemonade:
    Place bruised mint leaves in bottom of glasses before adding lemonade. Garnish each glass with a sprig of mint.

  • Berry Lemonade:
    To each glass of lemonade add 2 tbsp of crushed berries (fresh or canned). Berries can be raspberries, strawberries, blackberries, or blueberries. Garnish with whole berries and serve over ice.

  • Grape Lemonade:
    Combine equal portions of grape juice and lemonade.
